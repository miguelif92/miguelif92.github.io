Forestry Engineer / Geospatial Data

Reading forest landscapes through data.

Forest management · GIS · Remote sensing · Python · Web mapping

I am a forestry engineer and geospatial data analyst, currently studying Forest Information Technology at Eberswalde University for Sustainable Development in Brandenburg, Germany. My work traces forest stands from individual trees from the beginning of their life cycle until the tree mature consolation by monitoring all the activities and phases that are involved in the middle throughout the growth process, all of these by gathering data of interest for forest management directly from the individual trees as well as applying indirect methods and techniques through remote sensing products (such as LiDAR data, UAV images and satellite images) for analysing data and information that allow forest managers and stakeholders to assess and understand the changes the occur in forest for improving decision-making aiming on improving forest management.
