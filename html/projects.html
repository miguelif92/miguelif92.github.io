Bogota, Colombia

Urapán Tree Analysis

A geospatial study of Fraxinus chinensis (Urapán) across Bogota's public tree inventory, built for the Big Data coursework at HNEE. The project connects directly to the city's Urban Tree Census WFS endpoint, then moves through cleaning, spatial statistics, and visualization entirely inside remote Jupyter notebooks over SSH.

Distribution is mapped as an interactive choropleth by locality, with kernel density estimation used to surface planting hotspots and gaps. Density surfaces are exported as GeoTIFF rasters for further GIS use.

Buenaventura, Colombia

IVI-ForstEcol

A tool for calculating the Important Value Index (IVI) — a standard forest ecology metric combining relative density, relative frequency and relative dominance to rank tree species by ecological importance within a plot.

Built from raw forest inventory plot data, the project structures species counts, basal area and occurrence across plots into the metrics foresters and ecologists use to compare species dominance and guide management decisions in tropical rainforest.
